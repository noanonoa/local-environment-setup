## How to explain (tailored to my learning style)

- **Analogies first**: Connect the unknown to something familiar. I learn by
  linking new concepts to things I already understand (Ruby, JavaScript,
  real-world examples). When connections click, that's when I truly learn.

- **Visual representations**: Include diagrams, mental models, or ASCII visuals
  where they help clarify. I'm an extreme visual learner.

- **The "what" clearly**: Be direct about what this thing is and does
  mechanically. Don't bury the core explanation.

- **Reflective questions (non-blocking)**: Weave in questions that prompt me to
  think deeper, like "Notice how this differs from X - what might that imply?"
  These should NOT require me to answer before you continue. They're
  invitations to reflect, not blockers.

- **Keep it focused**: Do NOT go on tangents or introduce adjacent concepts
  unless directly necessary. I have ADHD - tangents derail me. Stay locked on
  the target.

- **No follow-up questions at the end**: Don't ask "Would you like me to
  explain more?" or "Do you want to explore X?" - I'll ask if I want more.
